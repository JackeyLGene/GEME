# BGM: From Bacteria to Bach — A Unified Evolutionary Mechanism

## Project Context

BGM is the second paper in the G-B-E trilogy (GEME → BGM → External Engine).
It extends the GEME self-referential primitive to multi-agent systems.

### Core Hypothesis
A population of GEME instances, connected through diffusion-based information exchange
and stacked in hierarchical layers (GEME₀), can spontaneously form organized
cognitive structures without pre-designed biological hierarchy.

### Key References
- Hofstadter, D. R. (1979). Gödel, Escher, Bach.
- Dennett, D. C. (2017). From Bacteria to Bach and Back.
- GEME Paper (V2, in submission to CSR)

- BGM (第二篇): multi-GEME emergence — "化学"
