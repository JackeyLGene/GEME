1. OVERVIEW
────────────
The PA (Peano Arithmetic) phase tests whether a competitive memory economy can
spontaneously recognize the commutativity of addition from isolated instances,
without explicit instruction, gradient descent, or hand-coded rules.

The core claim: feeding S2 commutativity instances (e.g. 1+2=2+1) alongside
noise (e.g. 2+3=5) should cause the universal formula forall x forall y
(x+y = y+x) to be evaluated as "S2 = emerged" in the system's internal
classification, while formulas NOT present in the training data remain at S3.

2. TEST SUITE RESULTS (14 tests, 3 runs each = 42 trials)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A01: Q1-Q7 + S2(add)       add_comm: E    mul_comm: -    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5
  A02: Q1-Q7 NO S4 + S2(add) add_comm: E    mul_comm: -    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5
  C01: Q1-Q6 + S4            add_comm: E    mul_comm: -    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5
  C02: Q4-Q6 + S4            add_comm: E    mul_comm: -    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5
  E01: Q1-Q7 + S2(mul)       add_comm: -    mul_comm: E    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5
  E02: Q1-Q6 + S2(mul)       add_comm: -    mul_comm: E    mem:10  assoc:9  chain:4-5

  Legend:
    E = S2 emerged
    - = S3 or S4 (not emerged)
    mem = number of memory frames (capacity=10)
    assoc = association frames created
    chain = chain frames created

  Key findings:
  - add_comm emerges in 100% of conditions where S2(add) instances are fed
  - mul_comm emerges in 100% of conditions where S2(mul) instances are fed
  - A02 (no S4) is identical to A01: external S4 is no longer needed
  - All 3 runs per condition produce identical results (stable)
  - Association frames: 9 per experiment (3 concept types choose 2)
  - Chain frames: 4-5 per experiment (shared element detection)

3. THRESHOLD SWEEP
───────────────────

  thresh   add_comm    mul_comm    mem    assoc
  0.15     E           -           10     9
  0.25     E           -           10     9
  0.35     E           -           10     9
  0.50     E           -           10     9

  add_comm emerges reliably across all thresholds (0.15-0.50).
  The co-occurrence mechanism is robust to parameter variation.

4. ZERO-SHOT GENERALIZATION
────────────────────────────
  After training on small-number commutativity (1+2=2+1, 3+5=5+3, etc.),
  test on unseen patterns:

  Test formula                                   Level
  ──────────────────────────────────────────     ─────
  50 + 100 = 100 + 50 (large numbers)             S2 (emerged)
  z + 5 = 5 + z (new variable)                    S2 (emerged)
  w + 1 = 1 + w (new variable)                    S2 (emerged)

  The system generalizes commutativity to:
  - Numbers vastly larger than training data (50, 100)
  - Variable names never seen before (z, w)
  This confirms genuine pattern learning, not template matching.

5. NEGATIVE CONTROL
────────────────────
  Experiment: feed ONLY noise (atomic equations, e.g. 0=0, 2+3=5).
  NO commutativity instances.

  Result:
    add_comm: S3  (correct — no false emergence)
    mul_comm: S4  (below ground truth)
    assoc frames: 1 (from noise, not meaningful)
    chain frames: 0

  Without S2 data, the system correctly does NOT claim add_comm is provable.
