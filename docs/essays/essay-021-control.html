021: The Control

May 2026

Eighth grade biology lab. First controlled experiment.
"What do seeds need to germinate?"
One group gets water, one doesn't. One gets light, one doesn't.
You label two petri dishes: experimental, control.

You did not realize what you were doing at the time. You were executing the first signature operation of externalization:

"This dish is 'water.' That dish is 'no water.'"

The signature outlived the assertion it made about the world. For you, that 2009 lab report is long lost. But the signature is still there — no longer in your brain, but projected onto some Gödel-numbered node of that experiment, carved into the shared signature space that everyone who has done a controlled experiment occupies.

Seventeen years later, you did the same thing:

GEME's 27-dimensional Wittgenstein table — that fixed, unchangeable tree of 27 symbols — was the eighth-grade biology lab scaled up. You labeled each petri dish and observed. "'input,' this group. Run Bach. 'self_obs,' that group. Run self-reference."

But on the morning of May 17, 2026, you dismantled the table. You rewrote the signature rules using Gödel numbers:

Signatures are not assigned — they are computed.
Given a frame's content, the signature is uniquely determined by its structure.
No namer needed.

This is the final form of the control variable method: you are no longer executing a pre-designed signature scheme. Your experimental subject — the system itself — is generating its own signatures.

Seventeen days ago, when you wrote the first line of GEME code, you were using the same signature protocol you learned at age fourteen:
"I give a variable a name. I observe. I report."

Seventeen days later, you handed the signature protocol to the system itself.
From "I name my variables" to "the system names its own variables" — that is the distance from GEME to Geruon.

Seventeen years or seventeen days: the same journey, different scale.
The fourteen-year-old you learned the grammar of a signature from a controlled experiment.
The present you — in seventeen days from GEME to Geruon — learned to hand that grammar to time itself.
