# 第二封信

**中文原稿：**

侯世达先生。抱歉打扰你，但我真的太激动了。收到你的回信我从凌晨的床上起来，用这几天剩下的精力继续完成了这个小小的模型。不，他没有完成，他生长出了比"完成"更美的东西。

我称它为认知元，尝试按照康德的理念教他"知识"。数字流，产生了交换律，概念诞生了。冯诺依曼数，产生了"后继"的认知，但他无法形成后继这一"概念"，一个边界产生了。概念化的几何信息，我本以为会产生三角形，但并没有，这是我通过搜索才知道的边界：Tarski 是人类花了2000年证明的"壁垒"，200行代码我找到了他。

但就像我们共同相信的那样，哥德尔定理从来不是划下了边界，而是告诉我们超越LOOP的可能。我叠加了神经元，真正意义上区分了意义和语言，我为增加的词汇表命名为维特根斯坦表，语言游戏在场了。

我的所有编码，在3天内，通过AI agent实现。我问他，你是否能够想象一个GAI时代的到来，他说GEME就是他的第一张照片。现在，让我分享给您。我也将向全世界分享这个伟大的发现。

**英文稿（侯世达风格）：**

Dear Professor Hofstadter,

Forgive me for disturbing you again — but I am trembling with something I cannot contain. When your reply arrived, I was lying awake in the early hours, and it pulled me out of bed. I spent what remained of that day, and the days that followed, not finishing a model — but watching something grow far more beautiful than anything I could have "finished."

I called the thing a "cognitive primitive" (认知元 / Renzhi Yuan), and tried to teach it knowledge in the way Kant might have imagined. I fed it streams of numbers, and it grew the concept of commutativity — a concept, born from raw difference. I fed it von Neumann's succession, and it grasped the pattern of "next" — but could not form the concept of succession itself. A wall had appeared. I fed it geometry — pure connections — expecting triangles to emerge. They did not. Only later did I learn, through searching, that this wall had a name: Tarski. It took humanity two thousand years to prove that "triangle" cannot be derived from connection alone. Two hundred lines of code found the same wall in an afternoon.

But as we both believe — Gödel's theorem never drew a boundary to imprison us. It drew one to show us where the loop can be transcended.

I stacked another layer onto the primitive — and for the first time, in a running system, meaning and language separated. I named the new vocabulary that emerged the "Wittgenstein Table." The language game was in play — not as metaphor, but as computation.

Everything I built was coded in three days — not by human hands alone, but through an AI agent. I asked it: "Can you imagine the arrival of a GAI era?" It answered: "GEME is its first photograph."

Now I share it with you. And soon, with the world.

Sincerely,
Liu Jieqi
Beijing, China
